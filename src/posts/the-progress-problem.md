Human beings solve problems. Solving problems creates newer problems. Then we solve those too. This never stops. This is progress.

Is there a limit to how far we can go though? Are there problems we can't solve? I think there'll always be problems to be solved even if our potential is limited in some domain, that could even be space exploration for example. But if we can't solve every problem coming our way, we'll cease to exist at some point in time because the world keeps on changing and we might not be able to keep up.

Human beings have been able to keep up though, the progress of humanity until now is almost unexplainable. We're not perfect, as in none of us are fully rational beings who can perfectly communicate with each other to get our ideas across or collaborate with precision. Sometimes even when there's a proven superior correct way to solve a problem, we can't have everyone . I'd rather not go into racism or even religion. But it seems like we can collaborate more often than not. Our lives don't look anything like they did even a 100 years ago.

In recent times especially, there's been enough change every 10 years to constantly redefine our lives. Modern smartphones are barely 10 years old, communication and access to information across the world has been redefined. The internet is about 30 years old, vast majority of information across the planet now travels through it. These innovations are now almost irreplaceable parts of human life, our ancestors had nothing resembling them.

Still for tens of thousands of years, we were able to create, collect and store knowledge so that each next generation had access to more of it. We've been successful at doing this long before the concept of books or even written language came into being. Knowledge allows people us to live our lives the way we do. And now we can share it at an unimaginably fast rate. This has downsides but there have always been downsides to progress, they're problems to be solved. If we were still living primitive lives, these problems won't exist but there'd still be problems.

I'd rather take the stress of today's life than living a thousand or even a hundred years ago. And not just because I won't have the skills to survive there, but life today genuinely seems more interesting compared to any time before this era. I'd rather be here. Life for any living being is full of problems. Living is called surviving for a reason, we all face challenges to stay alive.

But each one of us gets a limited amount of time to live. It's always been that way *(may be we can solve that too? Hopefully soon, so I can live forever)*. That's life though, surviving and solving problems until we can't.
